A Glimpse Into The History

Opened in 1962, Dodger Stadium has become one of the most iconic landmarks in Los Angeles and a cornerstone of Major League Baseball history. Built into the hillside of Chavez Ravine, it remains the largest ballpark in the league by seating capacity, with room for over 56,000 fans. Beyond its size, Dodger Stadium is celebrated for its breathtaking views of the San Gabriel Mountains, Elysian Hills, and downtown Los Angeles, offering a backdrop unlike any other in sports. Over the decades, it has hosted legendary players, unforgettable World Series moments, and even concerts and cultural events, cementing its status as more than just a ballpark.
