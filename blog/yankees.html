A Glimpse Into The History

Yankee Stadium, often called "The House That Ruth Built," first opened its gates in 1923 in the Bronx and quickly became one of the most iconic ballparks in sports history. It was home to legends like Babe Ruth, Lou Gehrig, Joe DiMaggio, and Mickey Mantle, serving as the stage for countless historic moments and championship victories. The original stadium underwent renovations in the 1970s but ultimately closed in 2008, making way for a new, modern Yankee Stadium built just across the street. The new ballpark, which opened in 2009, preserved many elements of the original design while offering updated amenities, ensuring that the legacy of Yankee greatness continues for future generations.
